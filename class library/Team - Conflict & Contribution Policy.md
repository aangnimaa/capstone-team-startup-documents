# Team Conflict & Contribution Policy

## Purpose

Capstone projects are completed in teams.

This policy explains how teams should handle:

- contribution expectations
- conflict
- freeloading

The goal is to keep the process **fair, transparent, and low drama**.

---

# Contribution Expectations

Each team member is expected to:

- contribute work every week
- participate in team communication
- post contribution receipts
- help move the project forward

Contribution should be **visible in GitHub**.

---

# Evidence of Contribution

Contribution is evaluated using GitHub evidence such as:

- Pull Requests
- commits
- Issues created or completed
- review comments
- documentation updates

Students must post **2–3 receipts each week** in the Sprint Packet.

---

# Signs of Contribution Problems

Possible signs of a problem include:

- missing receipts
- no commits or PRs
- repeated absence from meetings
- teammates reporting lack of work

These signals trigger further review.

---

# Conflict Resolution Steps

Teams should try to resolve problems using this process.

### Step 1 — Team Discussion

Discuss the issue clearly and respectfully.

Many problems are simple misunderstandings.

---

### Step 2 — Task Adjustment

The team may reassign tasks or clarify responsibilities.

---

### Step 3 — Document the Issue

If the problem continues, record the situation in GitHub.

This creates a clear record of the issue.

---

### Step 4 — Instructor Support

If the problem continues, the instructor may intervene.

Possible actions include:

- redefining responsibilities
- monitoring contributions
- adjusting team structure

---

# Freeloading

Freeloading occurs when a team member repeatedly fails to contribute.

Evidence will be used to evaluate the situation.

Possible outcomes include:

- reduced credit
- individual grading adjustments
- removal from the team in extreme cases

---

# Professional Expectations

Professional engineering teams rely on:

- communication
- accountability
- documentation

Students are expected to develop these habits during the course.

---

# Key Reminder

The simplest way to avoid problems is to:

- contribute regularly
- keep work visible in GitHub
- communicate early if something goes wrong.
